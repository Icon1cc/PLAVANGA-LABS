# Client review drafts (not published)

The site does not show a quote until the client has approved one. Until then
each card falls back to "Review coming soon" (see `quote: ""` in
`src/content/site.ts`).

These are **drafts to send for approval**, not quotes anyone has given. Do not
paste them into `site.ts` until the named person has confirmed the wording in
writing. Publishing a testimonial a client did not actually give is
misrepresentation, and it is the kind of thing that gets noticed when a
prospect calls a reference.

## How to use this

1. Fill in the bracketed details with what the engagement actually covered.
2. Email the draft to your contact with: "Here is a draft quote for our site.
   Change anything you like, or send your own wording. We will only publish
   what you approve."
3. Paste the approved text into the matching `quote` field, and add the
   speaker's name and role if they are happy to be named.

## Notes on the copy

Each draft is written to sound like a different person: Andee is founder-ish and
talks about scope, Maruti is operational and talks about missed calls,
Ashtavinayak is plain-spoken and sceptical of software. Uniform, glowing quotes
read as fake to anyone scanning a client page. Keep whatever specificity the
client gives you, including mild criticism, since that is what makes a review
sound like a person rather than a marketing line.
